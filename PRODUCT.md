# Product

## Register

product

Note: shareable artifacts (the `/s/:token` report page and the `/og/:token.png` badge image) are brand surfaces; apply the brand register per-task when working on them.

## Users

People who work with AI chat daily (Claude, ChatGPT) and want an evidence-backed picture of how they think and how capably they operate. They install the extension themselves, run the analysis in their own AI session, and choose what, if anything, to publish. A second audience sees only the shared artifacts: recruiters, peers, and LinkedIn contacts who click a badge or share link.

## Product Purpose

Turn a person's own LLM chat history into a living behavioral profile with opt-in shareable badges, computed without their raw chats ever reaching our servers. Success: the profile feels true to the person (every claim anchored to their own quotes), and the shared badge is credible enough to put on LinkedIn.

## Brand Personality

Honest, evidence-led, quietly premium. The badge artifacts specifically: "modern institution", official the way a design-led bank or university credential is official, engraved restraint over ornament. The provenance line ("self-computed, not verified by us") is a feature of the voice, not fine print to hide.

## Anti-references

- Credly/Coursera-style flat corporate badges: ribbon icons, default blues, generic "credential platform" blandness.
- Clipart diplomas: word-art borders, fake wax seals, Old English type.
- Crypto/NFT aesthetics: neon gradients, holographic chrome, hype-coded visuals.

## Design Principles

- Evidence before assertion: every claim in the UI links to the quotes behind it; visuals never promise more certainty than the data has.
- Honest provenance: "self-computed in your own AI session, not verified by us" appears on every shared artifact, styled with dignity rather than buried.
- The user owns disclosure: private by default, publishing is deliberate, deletion is self-serve.
- Quiet craft over decoration: hierarchy from type and spacing, brand purple used sparingly as ink, not as wallpaper.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target yet; hold 4.5:1 contrast for body text in the extension UI and on share pages. Badge images carry their information redundantly in the share page's HTML (the image is decorative reinforcement, not the sole carrier).
